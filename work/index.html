My work

Currently, I study coherent dipolar energy transfer between resonant levels of ultra-cold Rydberg atoms, specifically, of the non-radiative exchange of an excitation among a large number of randomly distributed atoms. I conduct large-scale numerical surveys on the statistics of clouds with a large number of atoms N and compare them to results of established random matrix theories, Euclidean and stable random matrix theory, for instance. I also work on analytical treatments for the asymptotic limit, where N goes to infinity.

During my PhD, I worked on excitonic energy transport in disordered networks, in particular, pigment-protein complexes with strong dipole-dipole interactions. I employed evolution strategies to numerically optimize the energy transport in these systems. I got heavily involved in the ongoing debate on the mechanisms that drive efficient transport across photosynthetic complexes.

In the group of Paul Brumer, one of my main concerns has been the substantiation of quantum coherence effects in the control of atomic and molecular processes with lasers, i.e. quantum coherent control. To this end, I integrated coherent control ideas with concepts of quantum interferometry, in particular, the complementarity of waves and particles, suggesting the possibility to observe non-trivial quantum effects in new scenarios of two-color phase control.

Publications

  1. Excitation energy transfer in ultracold Rydberg gases

    TS, Thomas Wellens, Andreas Buchleitner

    in preparation

    Found that the nature of excitation energy transfer (EET) in a Rydberg gas can be controlled via the dipole blockade effect ⋅ Analyzed the ensemble-averaged mean-square displacement $\langle[\vec{r}(t) − \vec{r}(0)]^2\rangle$ ⋅ Studied the spatial distribution of the system’s eigenstates.

  2. Nonclassical interference versus classical nonlinear response: When is coherent control truly quantum?

    TS, Paul Brumer

    in preparation

    Addressed the long standing issue of the significance of quantum coherence and nonclassical effects in scenarios of coherent phase control ⋅ Showed that two-color weak-field phase control, the archetype of the all-optical control methods, is an analogue of a purely classical control phenomenon ⋅ Introduced a new, unifying framework called the coherent control interferometer ⋅ Studied quantitative wave-particle complementarity in the setting of coherent phase control ⋅ Designed two new quantum coherent control scenarios: quantum erasure and quantum delayed choice coherent control ⋅ Showed that the nonclassicality and the genuine randomness of these new scenarios can be certified by Bell inequality tests.

  3. Spectral backbone of excitation transport in ultracold Rydberg gases

    TS, Thomas Wellens, Andreas Buchleitner

    Phys. Rev. A 90, 063415 ⋅ Published: 9 December 2014 ⋅ DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevA.90.063415 ⋅ pdf

    Studied spectral structure underlying excitonic energy transfer in ultracold Rydberg gases ⋅ Found evidence for a critical energy that separates delocalized eigenstates from states that are localized at pairs or clusters of atoms separated by less than the typical nearest-neighbor distance ⋅ Discovered that the dipole blockade effect in Rydberg gases can be leveraged to manipulate the localization transition.

  4. Certifying the quantumness of a generalized coherent control scenario

    TS, Paul Brumer

    J. Chem. Phys. 141, 204311 (2014) ⋅ Published: 28 November 2014 ⋅ DOI: 10.1063/1.4902253 ⋅ pdf

    Showed how to display the genuinely quantum nature of a generalized weak-field coherent control scenario (utilizing 1 vs. 2 photon excitation) via a Bell-CHSH test.

  5. Quantum transport in biological functional units: noise, disorder, structure

    TS, Tobias Zech, Thomas Wellens, Andreas Buchleitner

    Fluct. Noise Lett. 12, (2013) 1340007 ⋅ Published: June 2013 ⋅ DOI: 10.1142/S0219477513400075 ⋅ pdf

    Showed that 3D structures characterized by centro-symmetric Hamiltonians exhibit on average higher transport efficiencies than random configurations.

  6. Disorder-Assisted Exciton Transport

    TS, Tobias Zech, Thomas Wellens, Andreas Buchleitner

    Acta Phys. Pol. A 120, 89 (2011) ⋅ Published: December 2011 ⋅ pdf

    Discussed the role of disorder for the optimization of exciton transport in the FMO (Fenna-Matthews-Olson) light harvesting complex ⋅ Demonstrated the existence of a small fraction of optimal, though highly asymmetric, non-periodic conformations, which yield near-to-optimal coherent excitation transport.

  7. The optimization topography of exciton transport

    TS, Paul Brumer

    Europhys. Lett. 96, (2011) 10001 ⋅ Published: October 2011 ⋅ DOI: 10.1209/0295-5075/96/10001 ⋅ pdf

    Showed that configurations of a random molecular network that optimize constructive quantum interference from input to output site yield systematically shorter transfer times than classical transport induced by ambient dephasing noise.

  8. Optimal networks for excitonic energy transport

    TS, Thomas Wellens, Andreas Buchleitner

    J. Phys. B 44, 184012 (2011) ⋅ Published: 28 September 2011 ⋅ DOI: 10.1088/0953-4075/44/18/184012 ⋅ pdf

    Investigated coherent and incoherent excitation transfer in a random network with dipole-dipole interactions as a model system describing energy transport, e.g., in photosynthetic light-harvesting complexes or gases of cold Rydberg atoms.

  9. Efficient and coherent excitation transfer across disordered molecular networks

    TS, Fernando de Melo, Thomas Wellens, Florian Mintert, Andreas Buchleitner

    Phys. Rev. E 83, 021912 (2011) ⋅ Published: 22 February 2011 ⋅ DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevE.83.021912 ⋅ pdf

    Showed that finite-size, disordered molecular networks can mediate highly efficient, coherent excitation transfer that is robust against ambient dephasing and is associated with strong multisite entanglement ⋅ Offered an explanation for the efficient energy transfer in the photosynthetic Fenna-Matthews-Olson complex.

  10. Transport and entanglement

    TS, Thomas Wellens, Florian Mintert, Andreas Buchleitner

    Quantum Efficiency in Complex Systems, Part I, Semiconductors and Semimetals 83 (2010) ⋅ Published: December 2010 ⋅ ISBN: 978-0-12-375042-6

    Showed that excitation transport across molecular networks mimicking the FMO light-harvesting complex can be enhanced by quantum coherence on transient timescales.

  11. Entanglement witnesses from single-particle interference

    TS, Florian Mintert, Cord Axel Müller

    Europhys. Lett. 83, 60006 (2008) ⋅ Published: September 2008 ⋅ DOI: 10.1209/0295-5075/83/60006 ⋅ pdf

    Described a general method of realizing entanglement witnesses in terms of the interference pattern of a single quantum probe.
